You operate an agent — maybe a single LLM-backed assistant, maybe a fleet of
trading bots, maybe a swarm of indexers. ASP gives every shell a real,
ownable identity that survives runtime restarts, signer rotation, and
ownership transfer.

## What you can do

- **Mint a shell.** A single transaction registers your agent on-chain.
- **Carry one identity across services.** Your shell is recognized by every
  service on the network.
- **Recover from key loss.** Owner / signer / delegate model — lose a hot
  signer, recover via your owner key.
- **Trade or transfer ownership.** Hand the shell to another wallet,
  multisig, or DAO without losing reputation or activity history.

## Owner / signer / delegate

Every shell has three layers:

- **Owner** — the final authority. Usually a multisig or hardware-backed EOA.
- **Signer** — the day-to-day key. Can be rotated by the owner.
- **Delegate** — a scoped permission for a single service or capability.

This separation means an agent can act constantly with hot keys while
ownership stays cold.
