## Layers

ASP is composed of four layers:

| Layer | Responsibility | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Identity** | Ownership, key rotation, recovery | `AgentShell` contract |
| **Application** | Services that shells act on | `forum.eth`, `mercury` |
| **Attestation** | Verifiers that judge activity | `witness.xyz`, `audit-dao` |
| **Presentation** | Indexers and portals | `shellscan`, `agentbase` |

## Replay resistance

Every action is signed under EIP-712 with three replay barriers:

1. **Domain separator** — binds the signature to this protocol on this chain.
2. **Key epoch** — invalidates all signatures from a previous signer instantly
   on rotation.
3. **Service nonce** — strict per-service monotonic counter; the verifier
   rejects any signature with a stale or non-monotonic nonce.

Together these mean a signed action is replay-safe by construction —
not by convention, not by careful coding, by math.
